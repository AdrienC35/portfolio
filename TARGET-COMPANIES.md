# Target Companies — Short List Starter

> Guide recommends Series B–D + mid-size tech + enterprise newly investing in AI. Avoid FAANG as first targets — they filter career-switchers hardest.

## Filtering criteria (tier 1 candidates)

**Must-have:**
- Building something **agentic** or **LLM-native** (not just "adding AI chatbot")
- **Series B or later** (A is too risky, FAANG too gated)
- **EU-presence** or remote-friendly (EU time zone reality)
- Has at least 1–2 MLE/AI Engineer job openings right now

**Strong signals:**
- Public engineering blog discussing LLM production issues (not marketing fluff)
- Uses one of: LiteLLM, vLLM, LangChain, MCP, open-source ML stack
- Recent fundraise announcement with "AI engineering" in the hiring plan
- Engineers visibly active on X / LinkedIn discussing real problems

**Red flags:**
- Only mentions "prompt engineering" in JDs
- LLM features are marketing but no engineering content
- "AI Engineer" JD that's actually a ChatGPT wrapper PM role

## Category B — Mid-size tech adding AI

> Lower bar, higher chance of landing, slower moving.

Criteria: 200–2000 employees, existing product, recently formed AI team.

Sourcing: recent "Head of AI" / "Principal AI Engineer" hires on LinkedIn in EU. Where they are now = where the team is being built.

## Action plan

1. **Build list to 20 companies** over 2–3 hours (internal research, don't spend a week).
2. **For each: identify 1 engineer + 1 hiring manager** on LinkedIn.
3. **Rank by "excitement × realistic fit"** — top 10 go in outreach queue.
4. **Send 2–3 messages per week**, not 20 in one day. Steady rhythm.
5. **Track in a simple sheet**: Company · Contact · Channel · Sent · Replied · Stage.

Target: **5–10 real conversations per month** after warm-up. That's enough to have offers within 3 months if the positioning is right.
